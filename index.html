Managing AI Agents in Healthcare Security

INFO 492 - Team 6

Kat Khoroshilova • Jack Sliwoski • Kaibo Wang • Mina Hwang

Industry

Healthcare

Healthcare is a prime cyber target: sensitive patient data, zero tolerance for downtime. One weak link risks the whole chain.

Posture

Defense (Protect)

Our approach focuses on proactive protection and prevention, minimizing threats before they can cause harm.

Thesis Statement

The safest low-cost approach for healthcare email threats is an agentic screener that auto-quarantines suspected spam/phish into an IT review/training queue, minimizing user exposure and alert load without deleting or auto-blocking legitimate mail.

Key Constraints

"This isn't a complex hack problem—it's a human and budget problem."

BUDGET

Zero dollars for new security software

STAFF

Single IT Generalist (Director Li)

THREAT

Phishing → Human Error (Top Risk)

Design for the Protect Posture

Separation of Duties

One agent for risk assessment, another for the action of quarantine or delivery.

Guardrails

Agents stay in scope for specific duties. Strict whitelist for external tool usage.

Fail Safe Default

If an agent is unsure about an email's safety, it defaults to quarantine for human review.

Zero Trust

Analyzes all mail, including internal, to protect against insider threats or compromised accounts.

User Reporting

Provides a simple mechanism for users to report suspicious emails that may be missed.

Model Training

Uses human-in-the-loop feedback from quarantined items to continuously improve accuracy.

What AI Agents Look For

✓

Sender Checks: Failures in SPF/DKIM/DMARC, spoofed addresses.

✓

Headers: Strange sending paths, mismatched information.

✓

Links: Hidden redirects or lookalike domains (e.g., h0spital.com).

✓

Attachments: Macros, disguised executables, password-protected zips.

✓

Message Content: Urgent tone, requests for credentials or financial transactions.

✓

Context: High-risk topics related to EHR, billing, or scheduling systems.
